Max Temperature

Latitude is the angle which ranges from 0 degrees at the Equator to 90 degrees at the poles. Latitude is often used with Longitude to specify distances and locations across the Earth. As expected, there is a direct relationship between latitude and temperature. As one gets closer to the Equator, the warmer the temperature gets. As is the case, cities located between latitude -20 to 20 degrees have the highest temperatures. Corollary, the further away from the equator the lower the temperature. What is most interesting however, is the fact that the northern hemisphere tends to be much warmer this time of the year than the southern hemishpere.
